Alaska Income Tax Overview

Alaska has no state income tax.

Example: $75,000 Salary in Alaska

  • Federal tax: ~$8,200
  • Alaska state tax: ~$0
  • FICA (SS + Medicare): ~$5,738
  • Annual take-home: ~$61,062

Living and Working in Alaska

Alaska residents should consider the overall tax burden when evaluating take-home pay. Alaska has **no state income tax**, which means your paycheck only faces federal taxes and FICA. This is a significant advantage compared to states like California or New York. Social Security benefits are also not taxed in Alaska, benefiting retirees. The state sales tax rate is 0%.

Tips for Alaska Residents

  • Maximize pre-tax deductions — 401(k) and HSA contributions reduce your federal taxable income.
  • Check your withholding — Use the IRS W-4 calculator and your state's withholding form to avoid overpaying or underpaying.
  • Enjoy the tax advantage — Alaska's lack of state income tax means more money in each paycheck compared to most states.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the state income tax rate in Alaska?

Alaska has no state income tax. This is on top of the federal income tax which ranges from 10% to 37%.

How much will I take home in Alaska?

On a $75,000 salary in Alaska, your approximate bi-weekly take-home pay is $2,349 after all taxes and deductions. Use the calculator above for your exact situation.

Are there local taxes in Alaska?

Some cities and counties impose additional income taxes. Check with your local tax authority for details specific to your area within Alaska.
