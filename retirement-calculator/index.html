How Much Do You Need to Retire?

The amount you need depends on your desired lifestyle, location, and healthcare costs. Most financial planners recommend the 4% rule: multiply your desired annual spending by 25 to get your target nest egg.

  • $40K/year → need $1,000,000
  • $60K/year → need $1,500,000
  • $80K/year → need $2,000,000
  • $100K/year → need $2,500,000

Tips to Reach Retirement Faster

  • Start now — Time is your biggest asset. $500/month at 7% for 35 years = $829K.
  • Max your 401k — $23,000/year limit (2026) plus employer match = free money.
  • Use Roth IRA — Tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
  • Keep fees low — Index funds charge 0.03% vs. 1%+ for active funds.
  • Avoid lifestyle inflation — Invest raises instead of spending them.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 4% rule?

The 4% rule comes from the "Trinity Study." It found that withdrawing 4% of your portfolio in year one, then adjusting for inflation, has a 95%+ success rate of lasting 30 years across a mix of stocks and bonds.

Can I retire early (FIRE)?

Yes! The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement shows that saving 50%+ of your income can let you retire in 10-17 years instead of 40+. Use this calculator to model different savings rates.

Should I include Social Security?

Yes, but be conservative. Social Security replaces about 40% of pre-retirement income for average earners. Check your estimated benefits at ssa.gov.
